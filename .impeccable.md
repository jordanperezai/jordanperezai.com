## Design Context

### Users
People considering hiring Jordan for AI consulting, speaking, or workshops. Business owners, founders, corporate teams who need someone to come in and actually build AI systems — not present slides. Also other builders and potential collaborators who want to see proof of work. They land here asking one question: "Can I trust this person?"

### Brand Personality
**Real, sharp, built.**
- Real: everything is in production, not a pitch deck. No posturing.
- Sharp: marketing background means he sees the business problem first, then builds the technical solution.
- Built: the verb is the brand. Ships code every day. GitHub graph is the proof.

### Emotional Goal
The site should make someone feel like they just met a person who clearly knows what they're doing and isn't trying to impress you — which is exactly what makes them impressive. Confidence without performance. Substance without showing off.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Dark, warm, weighted. Not cold-minimal. Not sterile.
- Stripe's confidence (every element earns its space) crossed with the warmth of a local shop that knows your name.
- Premium but earned, not decorative. Like a well-made tool.
- Color should feel intentional and alive, not just "developer green."
- The jacket photo energy: personality, texture, realness. Not stock-photo clean.
- Typography should have character — not just Inter (the default). Something with more presence.

### Anti-References (what this is NOT)
- McKinsey deck (blue gradients, stock handshakes, "digital transformation")
- Developer portfolio template (dark + green + Inter = GitHub starter kit)
- Thought leader page (arms-crossed headshot, "I help businesses unlock AI")
- Empty minimal (three words and whitespace — no substance)
- Generic AI startup (gradients, robots, "the future is here")

### Design Principles
1. **Show the work, not the words.** Every section should prove something, not claim it. If it can't be verified, it doesn't belong.
2. **Earned weight.** Elements should feel substantial — like they have mass and purpose. No floating decorative bits. No filler.
3. **Personality over polish.** The colorful jacket over the safe black tee. Character over template. The imperfections are the brand.
4. **One clear ask.** The site has one job: make someone reach out. Every design decision serves that conversion.
5. **North Jersey real.** Not Silicon Valley aspirational. Not Brooklyn cool. Grounded, direct, no pretense.
